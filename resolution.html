How You Can Help

Wildflowers


Plant a range of flowers in your garden so bees have access to nectar from March to October.

You can use the winter season to plan a garden full of nectar-rich plants that bees can forage come spring. Bees love traditional cottage garden flowers and native wildflowers, like primrose, buddleia, and marigolds.

Did you know...

The bees' buzz is a sound made by their wings, which beat 11,400 times per minute.

Sugar


A tired bee really does like a tiny hit of sugar (never honey!) Mix two teaspoons of white granulated sugar with one teaspoon of water and put it on a plate or drip it on a flower, to revive a tired bee.

Make sure to always use white granulated sugar. Sometimes you might see a bee lying on the ground and not moving, but it is probably just resting. So after you’ve given it some sugary water, just simply let it be(e).

Did you know...

The average bee only makes 1/12th of a teaspoon of honey in its lifetime.

Sustainable Produce


When choosing honey, try to go for something local, from individual beekeepers who practice sustainability. This way you know where your honey is coming from and can cut down on the carbon emissions used to ship honey to your local supermarket.

Did you know...

A honeybee can fly up to 6 miles and as fast as 15 miles per hour.

Fruit


Growing fruit such as strawberries and apples provides bees and other pollinators with pollen and nectar while the plants are in flower, then at harvest time you will have delicious fruit to eat and share.

Did you know...

The queen bees royal duty is to lay up to 1500 eggs per day.

Know Your Bees


There are around 200 species of solitary bees in the UK.

Get to know the bees in your garden and in the green spaces around you by downloading the WWF bee identification chart below.

Did you know...

A queen bees life expectancy is 3-5 years.

Please make a generous donation to towards either their Save our bees program, or towards one of their many other great causes. They're all life changing causes.
